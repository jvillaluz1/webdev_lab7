Additional Facts About Each Planet

Mercury

1 Mercury has the highest orbital eccentricity of all the planets, with an orbit that ranges from 46 million km to 70 million km from the Sun.
2 Despite being the closest planet to the Sun, Mercury is not the hottest planet. Its average temperature is 167°C, while Venus averages 464°C.
3 Mercury has the shortest orbital period of all planets, taking just 88 Earth days to complete one orbit around the Sun.
4 Mercury's day is very long compared to its year - one Mercury day (sunrise to sunrise) equals 176 Earth days.
5 Mercury has no atmosphere to speak of, with a surface pressure less than one trillionth of Earth's atmospheric pressure.
6 Mercury has a significantly higher density (5.43 g/cm³) than would be expected for its size, suggesting a large iron core.
7 Mercury experiences the largest temperature variation of any planet, ranging from -173°C at night to 427°C during the day.

Venus

1 Venus is the hottest planet in our solar system with a mean surface temperature of 464°C, hot enough to melt lead.
2 Venus rotates in the opposite direction to most planets, meaning the Sun rises in the west and sets in the east.
3 Venus has the longest day of any planet in our solar system - one Venus day is equivalent to 243 Earth days.
4 Venus has an extremely dense atmosphere composed primarily of carbon dioxide, creating a runaway greenhouse effect.
5 Venus has the most circular orbit of any planet, with an eccentricity of less than 0.01.
6 Venus's atmospheric pressure at the surface is 92 times that of Earth, equivalent to the pressure found 900 meters deep in Earth's oceans.
7 Venus is often called Earth's "sister planet" due to their similar size and mass, but their surface conditions are radically different.

Mars

1 Mars has the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons, which stands 22 km high and is 600 km across.
2 Mars has the largest dust storms in the solar system, which can last for months and cover the entire planet.
3 Mars has two small moons, Phobos and Deimos, which are thought to be captured asteroids.
4 Mars has seasons similar to Earth due to its axial tilt of 25.2° (compared to Earth's 23.5°).
5 Mars has polar ice caps made of water ice and dry ice (frozen carbon dioxide) that grow and shrink with the seasons.
6 Mars has the deepest canyon in the solar system, Valles Marineris, which is 4,000 km long and up to 7 km deep.
7 Mars has a thin atmosphere composed mainly of carbon dioxide (95.3%), with surface pressure less than 1% of Earth's.

Jupiter

1 Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, with a mass 2.5 times that of all other planets combined.
2 Jupiter has the strongest magnetic field of any planet, about 14 times stronger than Earth's.
3 Jupiter has the Great Red Spot, a giant storm that has been raging for at least 400 years and is larger than Earth.
4 Jupiter has at least 79 moons, with the four largest (Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto) discovered by Galileo in 1610.
5 Jupiter is the fastest rotating planet in the solar system, completing one rotation in just under 10 hours.
6 Jupiter has rings, although they are much fainter than Saturn's and primarily composed of dust particles.
7 Jupiter emits more energy than it receives from the Sun, generating heat through gravitational compression.
